# Getting Started
{: .no_toc }

Welcome to BlastCAD! This guide walks you through everything you need to begin creating designs — from opening the app for the first time to producing your first drawing.

## Opening BlastCAD

BlastCAD runs entirely in your browser — no download or installation needed.

1. Go to **[blastcad.com](https://blastcad.com)** in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari).
2. Click **Launch App** on the home page.
3. The BlastCAD workspace will load within a few seconds.

{: .note }
For the best experience use a desktop or laptop with a mouse or graphics tablet. BlastCAD also works on tablets, but a pointer device gives you the most precision.

---

## Creating Your First Document

When the workspace opens you will see a blank drawing canvas with a toolbar on the left and a properties panel on the right.

**To create a new document:**

1. Click **File → New** (or press `Ctrl+N` / `⌘N`).
2. Choose a template:
   - **Blank** — empty canvas with default units.
   - **A4 / Letter** — pre-configured title block.
   - **ISO Metric** — metric units and standard line weights.
3. Click **Create**. A fresh canvas opens, ready for drawing.

---

## Setting Units and Grid

Before drawing, configure the units to match your project:

1. Open **File → Document Settings** (or press `Ctrl+Shift+D`).
2. Under **Units**, choose `mm`, `cm`, `m`, `in`, or `ft`.
3. Under **Grid**, set the grid spacing and enable **Snap to Grid** for precise placement.
4. Click **Apply**.

---

## Drawing Your First Shape

1. Select the **Line** tool from the left toolbar (shortcut: `L`).
2. Click on the canvas to set the start point.
3. Move the cursor and click again to set the end point.
4. Press `Escape` or double-click to finish the line.

Try the **Rectangle** (`R`), **Circle** (`C`), and **Arc** (`A`) tools the same way. Each tool shows live dimension annotations as you draw.

---

## Saving Your Work

BlastCAD auto-saves your work to your browser's local storage every 30 seconds. To save explicitly:

- **Save locally**: `Ctrl+S` — saves to browser storage.
- **Export file**: `Ctrl+E` — downloads the drawing as a `.dxf`, `.svg`, or `.blastcad` file.

{: .warning }
Clearing your browser data will erase locally saved documents. Use **Export** regularly to keep a permanent copy.
